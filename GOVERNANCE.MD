# Lonelog Governance

## Overview

Lonelog is led by a Benevolent Dictator and supported by the community.

The community contributes feedback, proposals, documentation improvements, compatible add-ons, and maintenance work. The strategic direction of the core specification is set by the Project Lead. In case of disagreement about the core specification, the Project Lead has the final word.

This governance model is intended to keep Lonelog coherent as a standard notation while remaining open to community contribution and extension.

## Roles

### Project Lead

The Project Lead is the Benevolent Dictator of Lonelog.

The Project Lead is responsible for:

- defining the strategic direction of the core specification;
- deciding what belongs in the core specification;
- approving and publishing official new versions of the core;
- determining which add-ons are official;
- interpreting compatibility questions regarding the core.

The Project Lead should actively consider community feedback and aim to preserve clarity, compatibility, and long-term coherence.

### Committers / Maintainers

Committers or Maintainers are trusted contributors recognized by the Project Lead.

They may:

- prepare drafts and revisions;
- review community proposals;
- maintain repositories, documentation, and release files;
- help keep official add-ons and supporting documents up to date;
- advise the Project Lead on compatibility and ecosystem issues.

Maintainers do not define the strategic direction of the project unless the Project Lead has delegated that responsibility.

### Contributors

Contributors are anyone who helps the project through feedback, discussion, testing, examples, documentation, issue reporting, editing, or proposed changes.

Anyone may contribute. Contributions are welcomed, but inclusion in the official core or official add-ons is decided by the Project Lead.

## Decision-Making

The default decision-making model for Lonelog is discussion followed by Project Lead decision.

The community is encouraged to discuss proposals openly. Maintainers may review and summarize proposals. The Project Lead makes final decisions on:

- core notation changes;
- compatibility rules;
- official wording of the specification;
- official add-on status;
- release timing for the canonical core.

## Canonical Core

The canonical Lonelog core is the latest official version published by the Project Lead in the official project location.

Community forks, derivatives, and compatible add-ons are allowed under the project license, but they are not the canonical core unless adopted as such by the Project Lead.

## Add-ons

Lonelog distinguishes between:

- official add-ons, maintained as part of the official ecosystem;
- community add-ons, created independently and governed by the public compatibility guidelines.

Community add-ons are encouraged. However, compatibility with Lonelog does not by itself make an add-on official.

## Continuity

If the Project Lead becomes temporarily unavailable, an Acting Maintainer previously designated by the Project Lead may handle operational maintenance.

Operational maintenance may include:

- updating repositories;
- publishing non-strategic fixes;
- keeping official files available;
- responding to urgent continuity issues.

The Acting Maintainer should avoid major strategic or breaking changes to the core unless clearly necessary.

If the Project Lead becomes permanently unavailable or is absent for an extended period without prior designation, active maintainers may publish a public continuity notice and appoint an interim lead by consensus for maintenance purposes.

## Amendments

This governance document may be updated by the Project Lead.

Any significant governance change should be published together with the updated document in the official project location.
